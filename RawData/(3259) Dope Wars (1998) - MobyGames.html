Description

Dope Wars 2.0 casts you as a debt ridden drug dealer - $2000 in your pocket, but $5500 in debt to a Loan Shark. The only way to get out of this debt trap is to make money by buying the available drugs and then moving to a new location and hoping the price moves up, or is driven up by market demands. Along the way, you'll encounter various hazards in your filthy trade such as cops, muggers, loan sharks needing repaying, etc.

Ultra low tech, amazingly immoral, and incredibly fun

The Good
There isn't much to this game. It is basically an economic sim (they should've called it Narcotics Tycoon) that lets you try to amass wealth as a drug dealer while you fend off loan sharks and dodge the cops. You can't beat the price - it's shareware, and the registration is only five dollars. A typical game only takes about ten minutes to play, but you'll probably finding yourself playing it over and over again trying to beat your highscore.

The Bad
It's tough to be critical of a five dollar game, since the price point doesn't exactly create a great deal of expectation. I will say this - the old lady who pops up periodically while you peddle your wares can get a tad annoying. Fortunately, you can edit the phrases she spews at you in a text file in the Dope Wars root directory.

The Bottom Line
If you can get beyond the decidedly questionable morality of a drug dealer sim, you'll probably really enjoy this game. There is just enough challenge and variance to the experience to keep you playing over and over and over.

Windows · by Kurt Sample (1071) · 2001

Trivia

Inspiration

Dope Wars takes its inspiration from Taipan, where you play a trader in the ancient China Seas, trading resources from the mundane (silk) to illegal (opium), while surviving pirates, thieves, and the racketeers that demand protection money.

Another inspiration would probably be the 1984 interactive fiction game called Drug Wars. So similar in fact, you might as well call it (roughly) a total rip-off in the concept (there's got to be a copyright violation here somewhere, maybe).
