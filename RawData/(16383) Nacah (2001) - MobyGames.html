Description official description

Nacah is a Bible-based point and click adventure game. The perspective is first-person and the navigation is slide show, similar to Myst.

The player character is stranded on a group of islands and the point of the game is to find a way to leave them. Otherwise, the game has no plot. There are a few mechanical puzzles, but most of them are Bible based, and having a Bible handy is recommended in solving this adventure.

Nacah (or,should I say,Myst for Christians?)

The Good
The user interface is simple and intuitive,even for first time players,and sound is really immersing: music and sound effects will only play on certain moments, as the rest of the games is silent,while you wander in the dark caverns. Puzzles are clever enough to keep you engaged. Nacah is also compatible with newer OS(tested on Windows 7 64bit)

The Bad
You cannot go back to previous areas,meaning that if you don't find all the pages of the Bible the first time, you won't be allowed to revisit the previous areas,making the game unbeatable. Also,some areas can be very dark and that's a shame,because i really like the graphics in this game

The Bottom Line
It's Myst for the Christians,or Myst with no plot. The goal of the game is to find 6 pages of the Bible and explore the caves,until you find a way out. All the puzzles refer to Bible verses,so i recommend "https://www.biblegateway.com/" for an online reference;just make sure to choose the "King James Version",as the game's puzzles are based on that version.

Windows · by Rosario Zago (15) · 2015
