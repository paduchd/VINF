Description official description

World's Scariest Police Chases is an open world driving game similar to Driver 2. The player joins Sheriff Jon Bunnell in high speed police chases, inconspicuously tailing criminals and high way shoot outs.

The game takes place in a fictional city called Ashland. Players can freely patrol the vast city environment or take on a series of twenty missions. The first four missions tutorial levels, the rest of the missions involve taking on increasingly difficult criminals. Criminals range from a simple DUI or car thief to drug dealers, bank robbers and even a runaway tank.

Once you reach a criminal you must either pursue them long enough until they give up, or ram or shoot them until they are forced to stop. A blue and red bar at the bottom of the screen show the status of the criminal vehicle. The blue bar indicates how long you've been chasing a vehicle, the red bar is the damage indicator of the criminal vehicle.

A two-player co-operative mode exists, in which one player drives and the other shoots. There are hidden areas of the city to unlock, such as an airport and a church. Hidden came modes include a Halloween re-depiction and a low-gravity mode.

A mediocre, slightly dull, yet fun copper-sim

The Good
I liked WSPC's idea of using real life missions in a made up environment (to the best of my knowledge, Ashland doesn't exist, but if it does, please tell me) makes this game an exciting enough driving sim, with it's wide variety of cars (including my favourite - the bus) and a seemingly unlimited arsenal of weaponry, it's a very good game.

The Bad
I hated the way that the game's commentator didn't over-exaggerate, and by not using key phrases like "Oh my god he nearly hit that pedestrian," "He's attacking the officers," and "Oh my god!!!! He almost collided with another car!" What a pity. The other thing was the fact that the suspects can escape to easily,and the car easily sustains large amounts of damage.

The Bottom Line
A game not for the feint hearted. Get it by all means, but only if you're up to the challenge.

PlayStation · by Fos (12) · 2002

Trivia

Cancelled versions

A PC version was announced but never completed and cancelled. A sequel by Hotgen was in the works but cancelled. Only some copies of the pre-release disc exist. However, the game was originally planned to be on the Dreamcast, but when the Dreamcast was dying, the development passed on to the Playstation.
