Description

This is the first Windows 9x version of the long running Microsoft Flight Simulator series. Featuring two new planes, the Boeing 737-400 and Extra 300S. Also new to this version are virtual pilot instruction, as well as a multimedia flight school, and photo-realistic satellite imaging.

The full list of planes available is:* Boeing 737-400

  • Cessna Skylane RG R182
  • Extra 300S
  • Learjet 35A
  • Schweizer 2-32 Sailplane, and the
  • Sopwith Camel

Scenery packs such as the the BAO Europe 1 Add-On scenery and those produed for Microsoft Flight Simulator 5.0 or 5.1 can be imported into this simulator.

Reviews

Players

Average score: 3.6 out of 5 (based on 6 ratings with 1 reviews)

Fly, my pretties! Fly far, and see the world...

The Good
I liked Microsoft's replacement of the oh-so-awful graphics and sounds that were available in MS Flight Simulators 3.0 and 4.0, which ran on an MS-Dos Prompt, with it's own VGA setup and your PC's internal speaker, emitting window shatteringly high pitched squeals. I also like the ability to be able to fly more popular planes, such as the Boeing 747, and some of the more unique planes, like the Sopwith Camel.

The Bad
I didn't like that fact that after the takeoff, you seem to stall a lot, and crash all the time. The inability to be able to control the movement of the plane with the keyboard made it even more infuriatingly pants, making you want to throw it away after a while.

The Bottom Line
It's a good game, but has been bettered and overtaken by other "more advanced" flight sims, leaving it behind, but not forgotten. It's good for the newbie players, but may infuriate them from time to time.

Windows · by Fos (12) · 2002
