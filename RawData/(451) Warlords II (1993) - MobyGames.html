Description official description

Warlords II is a 2D turn-based strategy game set in a fantasy world. The player is a commander from one of several races (like the people from the Kingdoms, the Dark Elves, Ussyrian Orcs, Sirians etc.). The player starts with one city, and must capture other cities (enemy or neutral) to produce new units. The creation of a new unit itself takes a certain amount of turns. The player organizes warriors into "armies" (groups of units, shown as one icon on the map, like the heroes in the Heroes of Might and Magic series) and lead them into battles. The units differ in the number of movement points they possess, but the armies are only as fast as the slowest unit in them. Battles are resolved automatically, the player cannot affect them.

Average score: 3.8 out of 5 (based on 22 ratings with 1 reviews)

All time classic - fantastic gameplay, quality graphics for its time and Australian.

The Good
The swag of different units, the awesome soundtrack, the increase in the heroes abilities and bonuses as time goes on and the detail in the scenarios. The terrain movement bonuses of the various units makes forces you to combine strength and movement in a stack of armies. The AI was also quite astute. And did I mention the soundtrack?? The names of the kingdoms were great, especially the Balad Naran - curse their evil hides!

The Bad
The main downer about Warlords II is the combat - a pure simulation, where you often get ridiculous outcomes like a dragon getting flamed by a light infantry, or even a scout. The diplomacy function never really worked all that well - you pretty much always ended up as running dog, and the fact that allies could not combine armies.

The Bottom Line
Stupidly addictive - really, after getting sick of other games, for years I turned back to Warlords II until it wouldn't work on the new computer...

DOS · by Glenn Fischmann (4) · 2001

Trivia

You can use maps created with the Warlords II Scenario Builder with your normal version of Warlords II, but you have to update it to version 1.11 first. The patch (~900 kbytes) also improves some game features and adds two new terrain types for random maps: Ice World and Mudflats. Look for the patch on download sites like Games Domain.
