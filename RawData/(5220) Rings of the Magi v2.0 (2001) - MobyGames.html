Description

When it was first released, this addictive puzzle game was chosen by Computer Gaming World as the Best Puzzle game of the Year. A game for all levels of puzzle fanatics - simple to learn but challenging to master, many options and difficulty levels. The mystics of Ac'Rained have crafted their rings to prevent the life force of their world from draining away, but now their secret art is in danger of being lost. As their apprentice, you are called upon to learn and harness the power of the rings. By matching up rings of similar colors their magic is released and the puzzle is solved. Advanced players will discover more intricate combinations and higher-scoring opportunities, providing additional replay value. True-color graphics and original digital music. 300 puzzles in full version, plus an editor to create your own puzzles. Two free bonus games also hidden within, can you find them?

Nice twist on a match-the-colors puzzle

The Good
This is a fresh twist on the old match-the-colors puzzle game genre. You have to match up rings of the same color in an attempt to remove them all from the playing board. But instead of directly moving the rings you "push" them with arrow buttons scattered throughout the board. Makes for some interesting tricks, as your arrow push may affect more than just the single ring you're trying to move. Wide variety of puzzles, some can be solved in only a few moves, while the more difficult ones might require dozens and dozens of moves. Very high "production value" throughout the graphics and sound. Good replay value due to the stats that are kept, you can replay any puzzle to improve your score or number of moves. Very handy "solve" feature that shows a possible solution for every puzzle - you can usually do better than this solution, so it gives you something to shoot for too.

The Bad
However, it's probably not for everyone. First, you have to be a fan of puzzle games. It's not as arcade-ish as Tetris and isn't timed, so it's not going to be a crossover hit with the arcade fans. There's more thinking and less action in this game. Second, some people might miss being able to move the rings directly, it takes a moment to get used to it after playing so many other puzzle games where they're designed like that. You can't do that here, it's all about using the arrow buttons. Once you "get it" though, it's a lot of fun.

The Bottom Line
Fun easy to learn puzzle game with lots of variety.

Windows · by Jill Bellamy (9) · 2001
