Description

In the spirit of past collections (such as Action 52, Cassette 50 and endless "Hong Kong special" pirate cartridge compilations) of entertainingly defective and charmingly terrible games, a call went out to solicit mass quantities of the flimsiest games possible, taking up the torch in pure Ed Wood style and sometimes basing games entirely around the expropriation of public domain clip art and sample tutorial game assets. In 48 hours, 17 designers threw together over a hundred simple games (most using Clickteam's free Klik & Play engine) many of which are even playable! Games include:

Trivia

Glitches

As reported by its developers, Super Corporate Executive Quest II HD Turbo Remix Deluxe: Return To Lord Skullington's Castle "cannot be run from the menu because its name is too goddamn long."

This can be circumvented by opening SpindleyQ/Corporate/Game.txt and editing the file to reflect a shorter, less menu-breaking name.

Inspiration

The compilation of 100-in-one Klik & Play Pirate Kart was inspired by the TIGSource B-Game competition.
