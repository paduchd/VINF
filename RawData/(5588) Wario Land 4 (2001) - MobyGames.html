Description official descriptions

In this fourth episode of the adventures of Mario's nemesis, the greedy Wario reads in a newspaper about a secret treasure lost in a pyramid. That's enough for Wario: he jumps in his car, enters the pyramid, and then you take control of him. Wario is a classic platform game with a twist: some enemies don't actually hurt you but change you into various forms, like Floating Wario, Zombie Wario, or Bat Wario.

Average score: 4.0 out of 5 (based on 33 ratings with 1 reviews)

great platform game. A bit short though

The Good
Graphics are very cool. Nothing especially spectacular, but they're done with style. The sound is quite amazing for a GBA game : there even is digitized speech. The tunes are good enough to be remembered. Gameplay is quite faultless : super level design, amazing. And I thought you couldn't do anything new with 2D platform games. How wrong was I ! And you got to fight great bosses !

The Bad
While the level design was great, it is a minor annoyance that the aim of a level is not to reach the exit but to collect 4 pieces of jewel, find the key, hit the portal switch and get back to the portal in a time limit. Miss a jewel or the key and you have to replay the level or you can't beat the game. Usually, it isn't very difficult to collect everything in the first run, but a couple of levels will frustrate you : "Where the h is that d jewel piece ?" will you scream.

Also, even with the occasional frustration the game can be finished in less that 10 hours, which is too short for me. Of course, you can play the game on hard mode or unlock a couple of secrets, but this isn't enough to keep the replay factor high

The Bottom Line
Warioland 4 is a very good 2D platform game, technically impressive. Level design is amazing. It's just too bad you can complete it in less than 10 hours.

Game Boy Advance · by Xa4 (300) · 2002
