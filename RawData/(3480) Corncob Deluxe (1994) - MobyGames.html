Description

Corncob Deluxe takes place in an alternative history where World War II never happened. But all is not well. Instead of fighting Nazis, the world must deal with an even more deadly enemy...aliens.

You take control of the Corsair "Corncob" and fight off the invaders in this sci-fi air-combat simulation.

Good fun if you can find a copy

The Good
Nearly everything. The graphics, dispite being in EGA (If I remember correctly) and not even having the the shadowing system that games with the LHX Engine had, fit the mood of the game quite well and generally look cool. The in-flight physics are as solid as a rock, although some provisions are made (such as firing rocket boosters on a F4U Corsair and not having the wings rip off as you approach the speed of sound, and a F-117A Nighthawk that can launch itself into orbit). Also, the game has the "suspension of disbelief" quality to it (at least with me it does), which is always good. The aspect of being able to get out of your plane and take the battle to the ground (literally) is excellent, and it's very fun to jump out of your plane and parachute. The plane is very easy to fly, and it only took me a fairly short amount of time to master the controls.

As for sound, it is distinctivly better than that of LHX Engine games even when they actually do recognize your sound card, but I think that the sound and music (music being something Corncob lacks) in Red Baron beats it out by a noticeable margin. Still, the in-flight sound very well done, and is therefore a plus. The last thing that I can think of off the top of my head is that if you attack your base or it's personnel, you get sent to prison for a set amount of time, and you can't use that pilot until the that time has pased. And that's in real life time. So, if it says that you can't use your pilot until next Saturday, you really can't use him until next Saturday (of course, you can always create another pilot to fly around with in the meantime)! Although some may see this as a negitive, I happen to think this is pretty cool, adding to the suspension of disbelief.

The Bad
Wayyyy too short. Only three full length campaigns and one with a single mission. And about four to six or seven missions to the full lentgh ones. Most of them can be finished if you have 10 minutes to spare, although some of them can be quite difficult to complete, thus taking up more of your time.

The Bottom Line
Corncob 3D/Deluxe is an early flight sim where WW2 never happened because Hitler had a fatal injury in his youth. However, in it's place, aliens have decided to invade Earth (again) because they need someone to clean their toilets (not really a joke). However, dispite the fact that they can travel light years in a short span of time, they can only seem to muster projectile weapons in their invasion, and a simple 500 lbs. bomb can blow up most of their structures. Therefore, they have been unable to completely take over as of yet. Also, out of all of the planes that were developed during World War II, the only one to make it into this alternate universe is F4U Corsair. And this sets the stage for you. You come on to the scene as a pilot fighting against the aliens, based at locations dangerously near the front lines. In Corncob Deluxe, these locations include a grassland, a wasteland, an area in which missions can only be flown at night, and the aircraft testing area at White Sands, New Mexico. If I remember correctly, there are two other unique campaigns in Corncob 3D, the shareware edition of this game, if I remember correctely.

As far as gameplay goes, the missions are pretty unique and varied most of the time. Although most of the mission objectives involve blowing stuff up, the location and manner that you must blow them up in is quite diffirent. For example, in one mission, you must fly to the highest point your Corsair can go to attack an alien power plant. In another mission, the power plant you must destroy is invulreable to normal weapons, so you must hit it with your plane to destroy it (and eject just a moment before it hits the facilty). In another mission, you must fly into a teleportational gate that's near heavy amounts of alien defences.

Corncob Deluxe is probably impossible to find in a store these days, but if you do see a copy of it, pick it up. If can find Corncob 3D (which I believe is shareware) on the 'net somewhere, download that, too, even if it's because it probably won't be up anywhere much longer. Most of the links to download Corncob 3D seem to be dead, so it will be a tall order for you to download it. If you do manage to find a link, though, then get it. You won't be sorry.
