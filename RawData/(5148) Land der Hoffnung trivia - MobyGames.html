Trivia

Development

The whole game was programmed in Borland Delphi.

Soundtrack

Land der Hoffnung was German newcomer Innonics’ first game.

The crew around managing director Marc Möhring, as enthusiastic as it was green, came up with a special idea to promote their debut program: they recorded a professional pop single. The song, written by Land der Hoffnung’s musician Matthias Preis and performed by musical singer Katharina Debus, was produced by Heiner Lürig – a renowned guitarist and songwriter for German “intellectual rocker” Heinz-Rudolf Kunze (”Dein ist mein ganzes Herz”). The result was the pop ballad Land of Hope, a cheesy yet likeable little tune.

Innonics produced a couple hundred single CDs featuring Land of Hope (4’12) and an almost identical Percussion Edit (4’12) and spread them among business partners and the press, with little to no impact. If the company had secretly hoped for a commercial success, it must have been utterly disappointed. With the PR effect of the song close to nil, Innonics had effectively wasted a lot of money – and all for what may safely be considered one of the worst German games.
