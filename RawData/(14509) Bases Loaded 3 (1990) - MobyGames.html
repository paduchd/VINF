Description official description

Bases Loaded 3 was Jaleco's latest entry into the popular baseball series in 1991. Changes over the previous title included slightly more detailed fielders, a new field perspective (home plate at the top of the screen, center field at the bottom), an editable team, and a new goal: to play the perfect game of baseball against the computer. Simply winning the game was no longer enough; the gamer now had to avoid common baseball missteps such as making an error and striking out, in order to earn a perfect rating. Against the very clever computer pitching, this could prove quite difficult.

Trivia

Credits

In the Japanese version of this game, sound composer Tatsuya Nishimura is credited as "God Of Soul", while in the North American version he is credited as "Music Lover". Although it is not known why this change took place, the most logical reason that can be found is that Nintendo ordered Jaleco and TOSE to change the alias because of their ban on religious references in their games.
