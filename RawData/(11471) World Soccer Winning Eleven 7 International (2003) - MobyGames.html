Description official descriptions

The third installment of the Pro Evolution Soccer series, with improved graphics and a bigger Master League. Because the Master League is so big, it's split into 4 zones with 2 divisions, and cup competitions in each zone. Other than most of the Italian teams, club names are fictional, as are many of the players. However, the game has an improved edit mode, so you can edit the team's name, flag, logo, shirts, and stadium.

You can use the 'shop' system to unlock hidden features such as classic teams. The game also has a training mode, and a challenge mode, in which you have to dribble around cones as quick as you can. Replays can be fast-forwarded and rewound, and the view can be rotated. The advantage rule is now implemented, so a foul will only stop play if the fouled team suffers from it.

One of the greatest sports games ever!

The Good
The new graphics make the game very realistic & improved gameplay too. The bigger & much improved Master League, is becoming more like the real McCoy. The Licensing is an little improvement over the last PES, with Some Italian teams & Feyenoord are real! So do the players names & likenesses, are improved too. The new PES Shop which lets you buy classic teams & players, and other unlockable stuff! Finally I also like the brilliant Trance music.

The Bad
Get some decent licensing!

The Bottom Line
A very realistic & an exceptional soccer simulation, if you have a Playstation 2 and what a brilliant soccer game, and not bothered about the licensing, GET THIS!

Trivia

1001 Video Games

The PS2 version of Pro Evolution Soccer 3 appears in the book 1001 Video Games You Must Play Before You Die by General Editor Tony Mott.

Cover

While football games usually have a famous player on the cover, Pro Evolution Soccer 3 features the characteristic head of Pierluigi Collina on the cover of some versions. Collina is probably the world's best known football referee.
