Description official descriptions

Destroy tanks, turrets, and other enemies in a future world controlled by powerful corporations. It's quite similar to Battlezone. Your play ranges through multiple worlds that feature different terrains, enemies, and powerful weaponry. The game also has network play available over modems and networks. Up to 16 gamers on both Windows and MacOS can play against each other.

Enjoyable Riff on Battlezone

The Good
This is a fun game to waste a few hours on. The plot is extremely simple (as it was for Reality Bites' second game, Dark Vengeance), but all that really matters is the arcade style and action. You get your choice of three separate tanks, one that favors speed, one armor, and the final a mix. You then blast through worlds until you are able to access the jump gate to the next level. A particularly nice touch is once an enemy is destroyed, 3d text with the score you earned appears above it and spins around. I usually would whine about a game with such vapid plot a derivative play, but it's just so enjoyable, once the techno music starts thumping, it's hard to get away from.

The Bad
As I said above, the plot and the derivative action. The plot's no biggie to worry about, but the gameplay does get stale after a little while. All the missions consist of is just blasting through various terrains, gathering keys, and then exiting through a jump gate.

The Bottom Line
A truly updated Battlezone.

Windows · by Adam Baratz (1433) · 2000

Trivia

DirectX

This game was one of the early DirectX/Game SDK games. You'll see in that Alex St. John, Microsoft's first DirectX evangelist, was mentioned in the special thanks section of the credits.
