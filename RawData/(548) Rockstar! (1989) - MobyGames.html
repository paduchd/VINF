Description official description

Rockstar is unlike any other game on the PC. Your goal is, strangely enough, to become a Rockstar. Through the course of your game you will have to produce singles and records, balance a hectic recording schedule with your drug intake while touring Europe and the rest of the World. Success does not come easy. An uneasy market, balanced with your own health problems leads to a very involving game.

As your success rises, so does the demand for concerts and public performances. You a forced to decide when its time for a break and when its time to go out and make some cash.

The game has a text interface, and thats all it really needs, as graphics would just complicate the issue. If you are after a graphics version, you may wish to check out Rockstar Ate My Hamster on the Amiga.

Reviews

Players

Average score: 4.3 out of 5 (based on 12 ratings with 1 reviews)

Rock your way to an early grave...

The Good
Rockstar has quite a lot of options to keep you interested. Naming your band, singles and albums all lend a person feel to the game, and allow for much replaying. Because the game is text only it leaves a lot up the your own imagination, like how your songs would sound and even what kind of music your band would play. This is good because it is unlikely in game music could capture what your imagination wants.

It is also VERY easy to spend a whole afternoon trying to beat your last score.

The Bad
It was VERY hard to balance staying off drugs, though this is part of the fun, sometimes I just wish my band could have got that bit further.

The colour changes could be bad for for those prone to seizures.

The Bottom Line
The game at its heart is a non serious simulation of a rock and roll band in the late 80's. Text only management might sound dull, but some of the situations your character gets into are funny the twentieth time around.

I had so much fun with this game, years ago. I want to be the best band in the world again. It is a wonderful feeling.

DOS · by Keeper Garrett (917) · 2013

Trivia

References

The game's introduction text starts with lyrics from The Byrds' song So You Want To Be A Rock N Roll Star .

Sequel

In 1998's issue #21 of the e-zine The Neo-Comintern, writer BMC interviewed Wizard Games developer "Sleepy Cat Summer" about Rockstar, yielding a surprising revelation regarding a planned (and long-delayed) vaporware new version, to be entitled Megastar:

"Megastar was never finished (lack of demand, lack of time etc), but it will almost certainly be completed before the Millennium (better ten years late than never?) - the latest delay is caused by- A) having to work long hours to scratch a living (whatever happened to Shareware?) - B) desire to convert the existing (incomplete) Megastar into a multi-player net-based game......

It may be shareware, may be freeware, may be subscription only....."

A further surprise revealed in the article was the disclosure of the "enhanced" audience-participation version of Rockstar -- in which the players would drink shots and smoke reefers in tandem with their characters.
