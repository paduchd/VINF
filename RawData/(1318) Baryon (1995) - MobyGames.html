Description official descriptions

Baryon is a straightforward vertical shooter which features a smooth graphical engine, a short digital sound track and various types of enemies. There are two types of ships, each with its own weapon types, as well as missile upgrades (homing as well as regular, more powerful ones), bombs and shield.

The shareware version of Baryon features only one level, but the registered version promises more action-packed levels with more enemy types.

One of the best shooters for any platform!

The Good
I wish I could get my hands on the registered version of Baryon... other than the amazing Tyrian, Baryon is by far the best shoot-'em-up for any platform, let alone the PC. It's that good, really!

Baryon has excellent graphics - smooth, slick and non-obtrusive, and great work has been put into making it one fast little monkey. Running at full 60 frames per second on anything from a 486 to a Pentium III, this baby rocks! It also utilizes a bizarre tweaked 256x200 VGA mode... go figure. Baryon also has some of the best music heard in a computer game to date. I only wish it was player at higher quality and was longer, but perhaps the registered version of the game actually sports exactly that.
With great gameplay, good replay value (for a 1-level demo!), excellent controls and overall blast-fest for the entire family, how can anyone go wrong?

The Bad
Nada. Baryon rules!

The Bottom Line
A great, little-known game which didn't get even a percent of the attention it should have got.

DOS · by Tomer Gabel (4539) · 2000

Trivia

Graphics

Baryon runs in tweaked VGA mode: 256x200 in 8 bit colour (which also causes problems in getting screenshots from the game).

Beta version

A (possibly leaked) beta version of Baryon didn't feature the music in the final version and used Tran's PMODE/W extender, as opposed to DOS4/GW in the final version.
