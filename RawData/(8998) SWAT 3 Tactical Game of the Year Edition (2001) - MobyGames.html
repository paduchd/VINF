Description official description

The Tactical Game Of The Year Edition of SWAT 3: Close Quarters Battle contains the game with some added missions. Also included is an Advanced Tactics CD containing educational videos shot on location at the famous Blackwater Training Center with commentary provided by actual SWAT officers.

SWAT 3 and SWAT 3: Elite Edition owners were able to upgrade to this version, except for the Advanced Tactics CD, via free downloads on the Sierra website.

Realistic SWAT simulation, lot's of fun.

The Good

You have to do this by using tactics and team work. You have to quickly identify if it is a suspect or a hostage, then make a quick decision to either shoot or arrest the suspect if he is holding a gun. You get awarded for saving/ arresting people and not for killing as many as you can. Itâ€™s not a Shoot-em-up but more like an arrest-em-up. It has a lot of realism but it also has a high fun factor. The game is made by developers with SWAT (tactics) knowledge and made use of real SWAT advisors and consultants. It has good mod and map making abilities which has kept the SWAT community/game alive for many years.

Do ya feel lucky, punk? Well, do ya?

The Good
After upgrading from SWAT 3: Close Quarters Battle (CQB), I was thrilled to see that you could now create your own custom missions, without having to log on to the online servers and be the only sad, lonely person playing a one-man co-operative. I can honestly say that SWAT 3 is one of the best first person shooters I've played on. It's also very, very addictive, and the online content now is brilliant.

The Bad
I'm a bit miffed at the fact that you can't find mod files for SWAT as easily as you can for games like Half-Life/Counter-Strike/Team Fortress. The other niggly thing is that I can't get WorldCraft to load my custom levels, so that I can run them in the game. Maybe I'm going wrong somewhere. In all, it is a great game.

The Bottom Line
This is an excellent addition to any shoot-em up lover's game collection, so do the right thing, and donate your money to the smiling man in the computer game shop. Programmers have to eat too, you know...
