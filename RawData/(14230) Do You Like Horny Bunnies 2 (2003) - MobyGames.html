Description official description

Working as a sommelier in a restaurant in Italy, Kazuma Takatsuki gets a surprise visitor - Sumika Ichikawa, the girl who broke his heart back in college. He was indecisive about his future and unwilling to commit to her, and she thought it better to end things than to continue in a directionless relationship. Seven years have changed them both greatly. He is now a reliable man, and a master of wine selection, paid well for his talents. She has a good mind for business, is to manage the soon-to-open second branch of the famous members-only Italian restaurant, "Platinum". She is also married. Sumika convinces Kazuma to move back to Japan and work for her, and he can't pass the offer up.

In addition to being a top-flight restaurant, "Platinum" has another attraction; it is staffed by beautiful young women in bunny suits. As the bunny girls get to know Kazuma, they each begin to like him. Which of the girls will he choose? Or, can he still rekindle his old flame?

Do You Like Horny Bunnies? 2 plays as a "Choose Your Own Adventure"-type adventure game. The story progresses at its own pace, pausing on occasion to allow you to make a decision, thus resulting in a plot branch. The game features several different endings.

The game features graphic depictions of sex, and is for adults only.

Better than the first, but doesn't have much else going for it.

The Good
The biggest flaw in the original Bunnies was its poor character designs. In contrast, Do You Like Horny Bunnes? 2 features girls who are much more pleasing to look at. The only flaw here, and this may not be considered a "flaw" by every gamer, is that the girls all have extremely exaggerated bodily proportions (just take a look at the screen shots). This isn't necessarily a horrible thing, but it is a little laughable.

Like the original, Bunnies 2 also features excellent artwork. While the artistic value is not as high as some other recently-released bishoujo game titles, it is still well above average.

The Bad
Unfortunately, aside from the character designs, Bunnies 2 has all of the same flaws as the original. The plot is non-existent. There is virtually no way to tell which choices you should make to woo a particular girl unless you save, choose something at random, and reload, or use a walkthrough. And, the girls' individual stories are ultimately forgettable, as are the characters themselves. I played the game through when I originally purchased it, and again for this review, and I still cannot remember a single girl's ending.

The Bottom Line
Do You Like Horny Bunnies? 2 is a pleasant diversion, but ultimately forgettable. Somewhat better than the first Bunnies, but not enough to warrant a purchase.

Windows · by Eurythmic (2666) · 2004

Trivia

References

One of the girls in the game, Madoka Kasuga, is named in homage to the 1980s anime/manga series Kimagure Orange Road. A high school romantic comedy adored in Japan and in the US, KOR's principal characters were named Kyosuke Kasuga and Madoka Ayukawa. If Kyosuke and Madoka were to marry, Madoka's new name would be Madoka Kasuga. The Bunnies 2 character bears no resemblance to the anime character, except in name.
