Description

This installment of the Steel Panthers series mainly covers World War II. The player can command a single platoon up to a battalion on either side. It is also possible to construct custom battles and edit maps.

Well done update of a classic wargame

The Good
All the elements that made the original DOS Steel Panthers great, and now you can play it on a Windows 9x machine without all the headaches associated with trying to make DOS games work.

The Bad
Graphics are stunning for a wargame, but serviceable at best when compared to the industry standard. I realize that graphics aren't really the point of wargames, but my aesthetic sense pines for a wargame with deep gameplay AND beauty.

The Bottom Line
Steel Panthers is perhaps the best WWII wargame ever made.

Windows · by Kurt Sample (1071) · 2001

One of the best turn based strategy games

The Good
The detail of the scenarios and equipment. There is even a encyclopedia built into the game to explain what every unit is. The controls once mastered are elegant and effective. Incredibly detailed and realistic modelling of unit behaviour and results of battle.

Unit level gaming so, it has a gripping atmosphere, similar to Close Combat (remember this is a turn based game though).

Covers a wide time span from 1935 to 1949 so you can pit a post war US army against the Chinese if you wish. Dozens of accurate national armies are represented.

Runs from the HD without the need for a CD.

The AI is pretty strong and you won't find this game a pushover.

Turn based - so it will not appeal to everyone, but it's about the best turn based wargame for the PC around.

Powerful editor is included and there are loads of scenarios to download.

All this and it's free!

The Bad
Downloading the game is not so much fun if you're on a dial up modem. Version 5.0 is over 400mb. Upgrades from this point are more reasonable at a few megs though. It is worth it! Use the alternative mirror sites if possible.

The stereo sound upgrade should be included as standard.

The Bottom Line
Serious wargaming. Turn based realism from Matrix games, an amazing (free) game for strategy fans. If you like Eastern Front from Talonsoft or similar games, you'll really enjoy this.

Windows · by Felix (129) · 2001

Trivia

Originally this was an official project from SSI. When SSI cancelled the project, Matrix Game decided to give the game away free.
