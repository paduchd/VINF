Description official descriptions

Pac Man is back this time under the direction of famous Game designer Shigeru Miyamoto (The man behind Zelda, Mario etc.)

In this game one person plays as the famous Pac Man while the other players take the role of the ghosts desperately trying to stop the yellow menace from eating his way to a predetermined score.

Now the twist is Pac Man sees the game from the classic Pac Man view on the Game Boy Advance while to ghosts each have a limited view of the area in 3D on the television screen. Teamwork becomes vital as they try to hunt down Pac Man, the ghost who eats Pac Man then gets the Game Boy Advance and tries his luck as the hunted.

If Four players are not available computers will act as any ghost not directly controlled by a player. Computer ghost can not hurt Pac Man unless they have been touched by a human controlled ghost. Which ever human player touches them first will get control of the game boy if the computer catches Pac Man.

A Power-Pellet of Fun !_!

The Good
The uses of the GBA NGC connection have always interested me but I rarely have seen them put to as much good use as in this game.

However, here is a fine example of good thinking that just makes me what to craft a giant Pac-Man out of the most yellow substance I can find. The fact that your friends all are a ghost and the only way they can get more points to beat the others makes for a tense chase. Everyone wants to be Pac-Man and as pseudo-teamwork to catch you commences you just know your in for fun.

There is also an undeniable pleasure of leading your comrades to Power-Pellets ;---)

The Bad
Well all in all for a game that comes free with another game (That would have been worth it alone) there isn't much bad to say.

I suppose the graphics were bland and the music was, well... What you would expect of recent Pac-Man. You also must have a GBA-NGC wire which can be annoying if you don't feel like spending money on such a device (Oh come on people it's only $10! :-P )

The only thing that really gives me pause is the use of Mario as the announcer... Somehow the voice just didn't work.

The Bottom Line
A superlative multiplayer, should be in any GameCube owners library who is in the possession of three friends.

GameCube · by Opipeuter (17137) · 2004

Trivia

Pac-Man Vs. was available free of charge in North America when you bought the GameCube versions of R:Racing Evolution or I-Ninja. If you bought either of these games, the cashier, such as at EB Games, would hand you a copy of Pac-Man Vs. This was a limited time promotion, which has since ended. You could also get Pac-Man Vs. by buying the Pac-Man Vs./Pac-Man World 2 two-games-in-one-package title.
