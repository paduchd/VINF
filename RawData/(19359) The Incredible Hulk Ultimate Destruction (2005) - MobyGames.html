Description official descriptions

The Incredible Hulk faces against his greatest adversary - the Abomination - in a free-roaming, open-world action-adventure. Every object is interactive, plus you can also wield the power of 'weaponization', whereby you can create new weapons from everyday objects. As the most powerful creature on earth, take the battle from the city to the badlands, smash six classic Marvel bosses and finally defeat the Devil Hulk inside your mind.

The madder the hulk... the better the game

The Good
This game is a great stress relief and a lot of fun.

You feel like you can really smash just almost everything: cars, trucks, tanks, planes, choppers, people... You can grab a car and throw it to a soldier or the other way around: grab a soldier and smash him against a tank.

There are a plethora of special moves you can purchase by smashing things: every time you go on rampage, you'll earn smash points. Those points are later exchangeable for special moves, for example: creating a shield, ride it, throw it... or air combos, and devastating ultimate techniques that destroy everything around. It's one of those sandbox games where you have a very large map without loading times between areas, lots of enemies and a large quantity of secret items for you to be discovered. Once you finish the game you are given some more skins and models to play with: Abomination, Mr Fixit...

The history itself covers pretty much the Emil Blonsky / Abomination saga and sometimes I feel like "The Ultimate Hulk" movie was based on this game.

The Bad
The history leaves a lot of loose ends: Mercy, Ross, Samson, the Mission Directive... and centers itself only in Hulk and Emil Blonsky. It can be very, and I mean very, frustrating sometimes until you get to accomplish a mission, but, I like to see it as a challenge, not necessarily a bad thing.

The loading time when you start a mission... it just doesn't belong here.

The Bottom Line
This game lets you be creative being destructive.
