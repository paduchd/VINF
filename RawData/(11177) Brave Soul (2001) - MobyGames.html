Description

In this game, the player controls a young man named Rudy, who was was raised in the beautiful countryside of Souvania. Ever since his adolescence, a beautiful girl standing naked at a lakeside has been appearing in his dreams every full moon. Once he decides to join the Hunter Guild, Rudy heads into town with his dragon Shell, and meets a girl who strikingly resembles the image of the Moon Goddess in his dreams. This meeting will change Rudy's life and will drive him towards a daring adventure, during which he will discover a great mystery.

Brave Soul is an action RPG with adult content. During his quest the hero will meet many charming young women, and attempt to conquer the heart of one of them. The player manages a party of maximum four characters, directly controlling only the protagonist - companions are controlled by the computer AI, and their behavior in battles can be modified by the player. The game features "nostalgic" graphics reminiscent of the 16-bit era, anime-style cutscenes, and voice-overs for the main characters.

Rather boring, but not too bad for an h-game. It's not Diablo!!!

The Good
It is a console-style action/RPG game which is a rare breed by itself, but especially difficult to find in the hentai line of games (at least among the ones that have been released overseas). The previous reviewer compared the gameplay to Diablo, but I'd say it's much closer to Secret of Mana with mouse control.

The still graphics used for cutscenes and character portraits are fantastic. The opening animation is extremely professional and looks good enough for a regular anime series on television. The in-game graphics are decent. They are not bad, but they are certainly nowhere close to the caliber of the sort of popular console RPGs you find on SNES.

The music is simple and rather cliche, but fits in very well. The voice acting is excellent.

The Bad
The gameplay is fairly automated. The most amount of control you'll have is with your main character, who, even after progressing quite far in the game, is still going to be repeating the same thing over and over. The gameplay is nowhere near as sophisticated as Diablo's which relies a lot on a combination of skills being used (even if the same combination might be used repeatedly, at least it's a lot more intelligent than what you tend to do in this game).

The character system provides no control at all. You do not control how characters evolve as they progress levels: they're very fixed in how they develop. For a game that relies on hacking and slashing through hundreds of the same type of monster over and over, the lack of an interesting character system really hurts the gameplay if you ask me. This style of gameplay requires something more to motivate the character to hack and slash all day long.

Another thing that's lacking is a sophisticated item system. Diablo's addictive gameplay stemmed a lot from finding interesting and rare treasures and developing your character's skills and attributes. This game has neither.

The controls are probably the worst part of the game. You control your character's movement solely with a mouse like Diablo or Dungeon Siege. However, one thing that has a major impact on the fluidity of the controls is your character's inability to move diagonally. As a result, your character will be moving in blocky zig-zags all over the place. The lack of diagonal movement has a serious impact on the pathfinding and the feeling that you have precise control over your character at every moment.

The level design is horrific. You'll be wandering through big empty spaces, sparsely populated with the same types of monsters throughout with the same repeating graphics all over the place and little to no landmarks to distinguish one room from another.

The missions are fairly dull and uninspired. The way they are commissioned from the guild makes everything feel like a side quest with just the bare minimal setup and very short and uninspired endings to each one.

The Bottom Line
As a hentai game, this game has relatively decent gameplay, but that's not saying much. It is nowhere close to being as fun and challenging as Knights of Xentar or Cobra Mission. Knights of Xentar is especially superior when it comes to gameplay and it has far better writing as well.

To compare its gameplay to Diablo which is absolutely superior in every possible way would be a mistake. Making such a comparison is bound to disappoint most people.

Sexy Diablo.

The Good
Liked Diablo? Then how about playing Diablo with some hentai in it? That's basically Brave Soul in a nutshell. A nice little Diablo clone with a distinct animÃ©/hentai theme.

The gameplay is based on hiring yourself as a mercenary and selecting different missions to undertake, and as mentioned is based on the combination hack 'n slash/rpg Diablo popularized, with a mouse-driven top-down interface from which you move your main character and click on the baddies you want to squash. A collection of basic spells and potions can be used via mouse-wheeling and right clicking, but your basic role is that of a head-basher, spells and other sophisticated stuff is left to your party members. That's right, party members, up to 3 of them that follow your lead and are AI controlled but which allow you to adjust their basic attitude.

These party members are the most interesting aspect of gameplay, as they not only offer different supporting battle skills but also have individual abilities that come into play in the different missions you play. For instance, should you have the thief character in your party, then she'll be able to bypass all the traps in the dungeon for you, while the priest will be able to read arcane writings that contain the clue for finishing a mission. Most missions require a specific character to complete, but you won't always be able to take every character you want with you. Each possible party member has their own distinct personality, and while Caroll the sorcerer will want to come on board on any magic-related mission, she dislikes charity work, and she'll run scared if it involves ghosts or undead creatures...

This is something to take into account as this is a hentai rpg, and the underlying objective is to get it on with the girl you like the most. And please notice I said "girl". That's right, while you have the possibility to score with the entire female cast (which come in all shapes and tastes as in every other hentai game, and yes that includes your nice dragon pet and the cat-babe), you are only allowed to pursue one relationship per game. Basically you have to earn the love of your girl by pursuing the missions that appeal to her, and thus earning her friendship. Doing so allows you to trigger the "relationship events" that play out in specific locations of towns that mean something to whatever girl you chose, and the relationship will grow. No, those "events" are not complete hentai-fests, and in fact the nudity and sex is reserved mostly for the final events when you solidify your relationship (although there are some pre-love nudity and sexual situations every now and then). The development of these relationships are surprisingly well made, with Final Fantasy-quality character development with the added bonus of hentai content, a fact that is enhanced by the monogamic gameplay system. This system is also one of the nice ways that Brave Soul has to increase replay value, which coupled with a Chrono-Trigger like system which allows you to save your game at the end and restart with half the experience you had at the last end game, plus the Diablo-like overpopulation of secret and unique items, missions and easter eggs make Brave Soul the most replayable hentai game out there by far.

Finally it's nice to see a hentai game with good production values that include complete voiceovers, great SD retro graphics a nice front-end and interface and even an animÃ© intro sequence!

The Bad
SFX and music sound like crap, and while the hentai scenes are attractive enough, the character designer must have a fetish with large-breasted babes, as everyone in the game is DD-size... not exactly my cup of tea and certainly a rather "cheap" touch.

More seriously tough, the gameplay can get on your nerves. I'm not the kind of player that could put up with Diablo's eternal click-fest for items, and while Brave Soul sweetens the deal with babes and hentai, it's still unbearable for me. Finally, the story is hardly worth mentioning...

The Bottom Line
One of the most surprisingly satisfying hentai games I've played in a while, and I mean that from a gameplay point of view, mind you :P. Good production values, well executed ideas and enough replay value to put most other titles in the genre to shame make Brave Soul a definitive must-play for Diablo fans looking for something new and hentai fans looking for an actual game to play besides badly-translated interactive movies with porn.
