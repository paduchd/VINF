Description official description

In the past, this brick busting-thing was a way to clear space from mines. Now it's an intergalactic competition, and of course you've entered the contest.

There are 170 levels and 4 difficulty settings, each of which must be cleared of all bricks in this shareware game in the vein of Arkanoid. You control a bat at the bottom of the screen, which must stop a ball as it comes towards you and direct in back up the screen, which is where the bricks which need destroying reside. Helping you out are bonus features including extra balls, bombs which clear all surrounding bricks, and fireballs to remove blocks quickly.

A relax one-handed-game

The Good
Ricochet is the same as Arkanoid, you must get rid of all blocks in the screens to continue. The only change now is the graphics, the sound and a few surprises. You control your pad with the mouse, so it is a relax one-handed-game.

The Bad
But after a while it is getting bored and till then you don't even have played all of the levels. The difference between the difficults of the levels is in the speed of the ball. But during the game it can slow down or increase its speed, so there isn;t much of difference.

The Bottom Line
Don't misunderstood me, I like playing this game. But it isn't very special. Oke beautiful graphics and a few funny surprises. But I didn't play it much again when I finished it. Probarly the most of you will get enough of satisfaction playing the demo, which is for free.

Windows · by Buuks (197) · 2001

Trivia

In the main menu, you can play with the balls inside the "R" structure to the left by touching its sides. Try to insert all the balls in the most right-bottom part of the "R" and get a nice little morale boost from the developers.
