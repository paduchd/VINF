Description

Thousands of years ago someone invented chess. Ever since, various board and mind games were starting to pop out of the most unusual places, testing skillfulness amongst the competitors. At some point in time, when people became familiar with the term ship and a submarine, this game was created. This is a two-player version of a ship-sinking game (there is no option to play versus computer). Each player has a fleet of ten naval units at his/her disposal, which then can be placed on the board in various positions, depending upon their size (ships take from 1 to 4 squares of space on a board with is a matrix of 10x10 squares). After deploying their ships, players take turns in trying to sink the opponent's units with one shot per turn. The one who sinks the enemy fleet and remains on the sea, is the winner.

Developer Commentary

The developer of the game writes:

College Project

Dive! was actually my (MAT) college project for one of the classes (I believe it was for Visual C++ class). The course was continuation to C/C++ earlier course(s) and focus was on working with Microsoft Visual C++ IDE and window programming under Windows. While we didn't touch any DirectX programming during the course, I was more interested in trying to see how DX works (than playing with various window components and their actions like buttons, checkboxes, color wheels, etc.) and at the same time work with Visual C++ IDE so I tried to join pleasant with necessary. Due to limited time I only made a 2-player game (yeah, AI programming can be intimidating and time-consuming :). Even though there was DirectX 8 available at the time, I didn't use any 3D graphics nor advanced methods that exist outside of DirectX 5 or 6, hence the minimum requirement in the tech specs.

Ship Icons

As a long-time fan of Command & Conquer series, I drew inspiration from the unit icons of C&C Red Alert and C&C Gold. While for most of the icons I used black & white hi-res images of actual ships and/or submarines and then reduced them to icon-size small resolution, one of the icons is from C&C Red Alert cut-scene (the one with two ships passing on a river surrounded by snowy mountains), reduced to icon size. Not trying to make smooth small images, plus I was limited by number of colors (on purpose, can't make pixel art with too many colors), I've added individual pixel colors by looking how C&C Red Alert icons looked like (they're 3D renderings reduced to icon-size pixel art and in the same color palette).

Font

Font used in the game is comprised of actual all-caps Verdana (or some such font), fixed to same size (square size, and centered, so yes, letters like "I" will have more space) bitmap image where I tried to create similar effect as Indiana Jones title. But those bright orange-ish colors wouldn't fit well with naval thematic so I picked blue/green combo around white/grey base which kind of gave it a steel touch.
