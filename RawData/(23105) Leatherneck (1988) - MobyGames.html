Description official description

A squad of four tough-guy soldiers have to take out some foes in a jungle. As the game starts, four soldiers are depicted, with any who are not present to play killed immediately - a joystick adaptor is required for 3 or 4 player games.

Leatherneck resembles Ikari Warriors - waves of soldiers move down the screen shooting at you, and you must shoot them before the bullets make contact. You are armed with grenades to take out mounted turrets, or kill a wide range of enemies at once, but watch out for enemy grenades. There are extra ammunition crates and improved weapons to pick up - waggle the joystick to toggle which one you are using.

Unusually, your shots are long-range and take effect instantly. You cannot change the direction you are facing, instead having the ability to strafe.

Good for a few minutes, but...

The Good
Leatherneck is a clone of Ikari Warriors which came out on 16-bit computers, where you control up to four soldiers that have to blast their way through various terrain. One thing that sets it apart from Ikari Warriors that it does not include stages. You basically have to survive as long as you can, while trying to beat your previous scores.

The game is presented in a top-down perspective, and all the sprites are much bigger. Depending how far you go without losing all your lives, you get to see different pieces of terrain, some of which feature ammo deposits, ground-based enemies, and planes that have crash-landed.

A big plus in Leatherneck is the ability for up to four players to join in, using the joystick interface supplied by MicroDeal (who happen to made the game). This is essential because the game has a high difficulty where lots of soldiers go down toward you at once.

There is not much music to the game, but whatever music is in the game is well composed. My favorite piece of music plays when you view the high scores.

The Bad
Leatherneck is good to play for a few minutes, but it gets rather boring after a while. Your character can't shoot in any direction other than forward, and the enemy screens become tedious after a few seconds. There is no option where you can just have music only, because as I said, the music sounds really good.

The Bottom Line
In conclusion, this game is in the same vein as Ikari Warriors. The object is to survive as long as you can without getting gunned down for good. The graphics are okay, and the music is excellent. The game is fun to play for a few minutes, but the high difficulty indicates that it should be played with more than one person.

Trivia

Scoring oddity

This game can be played cooperatively with 2-4 players simultaneously, but weirdly shooting enemy infantry results in a 100 points scored when shooting a fellow player in the back results in earning the player a 1,000 points.
