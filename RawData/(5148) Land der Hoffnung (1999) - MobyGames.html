Description official description

In Land der Hoffnung (Land of Hope), you are the leader of the small country Breehn, which is in a bad trap - the number of inhabitants is increasing rapidly, but there's no more room for them to live. So you need another place to live. A wealthy place, a place with room to settle down, with large forests and much green land for your cattle. And you eventually find this land! So you gather your trusted council members around you and begin to lead your people to this fabulous land. But you didn't know that others wanted that too...

Land der Hoffnung has 40 missions, each with increasing difficulty. It features 40 different buildings, each of them training different units (25 in total). The game is not about war (in some missions, you even fail if you attack an opponent). In short, Land der Hoffnung is a typical Settlers clone.

There is no HOPE, there is no GOD, only DARKNESS in between

The Good
To be honest: I like to see my name written in the manual - it was my only job for the game industry. But I am ashamed that the product sucks so bad.

The Bad
Well... everything? I know this crap since the early beginning since I had the "pleasure" to beta test it. And I can tell you that the beta test changed nothing, they included even more bugs in the final release (they sent off the beta testers weeks before gold status :)

Okay, whatever, I should review this... it's a Settlers clone, alright, that is interesting since the Settlers is the definite game in this category. Land der Hoffnung got released at the end of the Settlers hype, so the timing was bad aswell.

So why is it that bad? Hmm... graphics aren't pretty - in fact, they are really crap. When I got the first beta, I laughed my ass off about the people on the screen which looked like walking trees. I didn't laugh when they remained like that in the final :( Ground plane detection doesn't exist, you can let them walk over water or use boats on land, it doesn't matter. Enemy detection? Forget it! Sometimes the intruder alert goes off when the enemy is just moving in his own territory, far away from you in the other direction :) The mouse controls are jerky and unpredictable, the whole game is pretty slow thanks to the funny programming techniques used here. The sound may be okay, but I think its boring. Its probably the best of the whole mess.

A big problem are the missions. The mission descriptions are often not very descriptive, sometimes even completely wrong. There are missions where you must not kill ANY opponent even if he attacks you - if you kill one soldier, game over. And no, the mission briefing doesn't tell you at all :/ We complained about this when testing it and were told that this is intended "so the player must think a bit". The manual doesn't say a word about this, too. I think this is really annoying since you cannot know WHAT you exactly did wrong - the game just says "Mission failed" and not WHY it has failed.

Well... thats bad enough, I think. Ah, the setup program takes ages to finish - I waited 30 minutes or so on my 1.5 Ghz Pentium4 machine :)

Ah, it should be possible to play multi player - but since the network code is broken... well, go figure.

The Bottom Line
"Land of Hopelessness" would be a more descriptive title. Forget it. Or maybe buy it since I am mentioned in the credits - but hey, even I myself sold my copy on eBay :)

Soundtrack

Land der Hoffnung was German newcomer Innonics’ first game.

The crew around managing director Marc Möhring, as enthusiastic as it was green, came up with a special idea to promote their debut program: they recorded a professional pop single. The song, written by Land der Hoffnung’s musician Matthias Preis and performed by musical singer Katharina Debus, was produced by Heiner Lürig – a renowned guitarist and songwriter for German “intellectual rocker” Heinz-Rudolf Kunze (”Dein ist mein ganzes Herz”). The result was the pop ballad Land of Hope, a cheesy yet likeable little tune.

Innonics produced a couple hundred single CDs featuring Land of Hope (4’12) and an almost identical Percussion Edit (4’12) and spread them among business partners and the press, with little to no impact. If the company had secretly hoped for a commercial success, it must have been utterly disappointed. With the PR effect of the song close to nil, Innonics had effectively wasted a lot of money – and all for what may safely be considered one of the worst German games.
