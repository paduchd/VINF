Description

Jetset is a text mode 747 flight simulator, possibly the very first made for PC. Its code, in BASIC, was published on November 1982's issue of the BYTE magazine.

Except during take off / landing, where the runway is visible, all you can see are the plane's panels and controls, which you can operate using the keyboard. Despite its minimal interface, Jetset is realistic as it relies on a good physics engine.

Reviews

Players

Average score: 1.9 out of 5 (based on 3 ratings with 1 reviews)

Flying a plane with no windows.

The Good
It's not as scary as it sounds. Yes, having no visual references turns everything harder on a flight simulator (at least for me) but that's just what makes Jetset interesting. It's like riding a bicycle with no hands. The only visible scenery is seen during take off, as you can actually see the runway represented in ASCII. It really adds some kind of beauty to the game - and practicability.

The Bad
Just like I said, the lack of visual references makes it rather difficult to fly. I mean, it's not hard to fly to your destination, but landing is real tricky. I'm still trying to find how to pull that stunt! It's very frustrating to perform every single step right until the very last moment, and then have to start all over. But then again, landing is usually the hardest task - not only in flight simulators, but also in real life.

The Bottom Line
Try this one out if you're a huge flight simulator fan and would like to test your pilot skills on a totally different interface. If you're an hardcore (classic) gamer, Jetset is also worth a look, at least for it's historical value. Casual players or people who are not into classic gaming will probably find it too difficult or just plain boring.

Trivia

Development

The game was written in BASIC on June, 28, 1981. It was later revised on February, 28, 1982.

Origins

This game is a conversion of a TRS-80 game known as 747.

Title

The title Jetset is actually an acronym for the Jet Simulator Electronic Trainer.
