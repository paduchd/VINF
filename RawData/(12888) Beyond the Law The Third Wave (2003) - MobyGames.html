Description official descriptions

Beyond the Law: The Third Wave is a squad-based, strategy shooter in which the player commands a covert team of specialists to stop a renegade criminal element intent on destroying the United States of America. In this world corruption is rampant, the police have been bought, and terror reigns - an elite force with authority beyond the law becomes the last line of defense. This mob must be stopped before the US capital is destroyed in a nuclear attack.

Average score: 2.5 out of 5 (based on 3 ratings with 1 reviews)

Mediocre but not terrible tactical game.

The Good
Solid game that simplifies the Commandos/Desperados-style gameplay to a more casual audience. Relatively accessible and no-frills.

The Bad
Very bare-bones, the gameplay is not very intuitive at first. Silenced pistol user and sniper are kinda overpowered compared to other characters. There's no music aside from main menu.

The Bottom Line
Third Wave tries to merge tactical squad games like Jagged Alliance (based around combat) with Commandos (based around stealth) - and I don't think it succeeds at either. Combat is pretty poor, and stealth is overly simplistic. There are no specialized abilities that make Commandos-likes fun, and there is no proper cover system and your characters have too pathetically small gunfire range to make combat fun. It's not as bad as some people say, but there are way better games in this style. But if you already finished them, it's not a bad time.

Windows · by Ada Mackiewicz (31) · 2022
